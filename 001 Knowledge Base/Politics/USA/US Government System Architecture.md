## Constitutional Foundation

The US government operates under a **separation of powers** system established by the Constitution, dividing authority among three co-equal branches with a system of **checks and balances** to prevent any single branch from dominating.

### Federalism Structure
- **Dual Sovereignty**: Power shared between federal and state governments
- **Supremacy Clause** (Article VI): Federal law prevails when conflicts arise with state law
- **Tenth Amendment**: Powers not delegated to federal government are reserved to states
- **Enumerated vs. Reserved Powers**: Federal government has specific constitutional powers; states retain all others

## Three Branches of Government

### Legislative Branch (Article I)
**Structure**: Bicameral Congress
- **House of Representatives**: 435 members, 2-year terms, proportional representation
- **Senate**: 100 members, 6-year terms, equal state representation (2 per state)

**Key Powers**:
- Make laws
- Control federal budget and taxation
- Regulate interstate and foreign commerce
- Declare war
- Impeach federal officials
- Confirm presidential appointments (Senate only)

**Legislative Process**:
1. Bill introduction in either chamber
2. Committee review and markup
3. Floor debate and voting
4. If passed, sent to other chamber for same process
5. Conference committee resolves differences
6. Final passage by both chambers
7. Presidential signature or veto

**Committee System**: Specialized committees handle specific policy areas (Judiciary, Armed Services, Appropriations, etc.)

### Executive Branch (Article II)
**Leadership**: President and Vice President (4-year terms)

**Key Powers**:
- Execute and enforce laws
- Commander-in-Chief of military
- Conduct foreign policy
- Appoint federal judges and executive officials
- Veto legislation
- Grant pardons

**Executive Structure**:
- **Executive Office of the President (EOP)**: White House staff, OMB, NSC
- **Cabinet Departments**: 15 major departments
- **Independent Agencies**: Hundreds of specialized agencies
- **Regulatory Commissions**: Independent regulatory bodies

### Judicial Branch (Article III)
**Structure**: Federal court system
- **Supreme Court**: 9 justices, lifetime tenure, final constitutional interpreter
- **Courts of Appeal**: 13 circuits handling appeals
- **District Courts**: 94 districts for initial federal cases
- **Specialized Courts**: Tax Court, Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims, etc.

**Key Powers**:
- Interpret laws and Constitution
- **Judicial Review**: Declare laws/actions unconstitutional
- Resolve federal law disputes
- Protect individual rights

## Federal Executive Departments (Cabinet-Level)

### The 15 Executive Departments
1. **State Department (DOS)** - Foreign policy and diplomacy
2. **Treasury Department (TREAS)** - Financial policy, currency, taxes
3. **Defense Department (DOD)** - Military operations and national defense
4. **Justice Department (DOJ)** - Law enforcement, legal affairs
5. **Interior Department (DOI)** - Natural resources, public lands
6. **Agriculture Department (USDA)** - Food policy, farming, rural development
7. **Commerce Department (DOC)** - Business, trade, economic development
8. **Labor Department (DOL)** - Worker rights, employment standards
9. **Health and Human Services (HHS)** - Public health, social services
10. **Housing and Urban Development (HUD)** - Housing policy, urban development
11. **Transportation Department (DOT)** - Infrastructure, transportation safety
12. **Energy Department (DOE)** - Energy policy, nuclear weapons
13. **Education Department (ED)** - Educational policy and funding
14. **Veterans Affairs (VA)** - Veteran services and benefits
15. **Homeland Security (DHS)** - Domestic security, immigration

## Checks and Balances System

### Legislative Checks
- **On Executive**: Impeachment, budget control, appointment confirmation
- **On Judicial**: Impeachment, constitutional amendments, court jurisdiction

### Executive Checks
- **On Legislative**: Veto power, execution discretion
- **On Judicial**: Judicial appointments, enforcement of decisions

### Judicial Checks
- **On Legislative**: Judicial review of laws
- **On Executive**: Judicial review of executive actions

## Administrative State

The modern federal government operates through a vast **administrative state** of agencies that:
- **Implement Laws**: Execute congressional mandates
- **Create Regulations**: Fill in details of broad legislative frameworks
- **Enforce Compliance**: Monitor and penalize violations
- **Provide Services**: Direct service delivery to citizens

### Agency Types
1. **Executive Agencies**: Under presidential control
2. **Independent Agencies**: Insulated from direct presidential control
3. **Regulatory Commissions**: Multi-member boards with staggered terms
4. **Government Corporations**: Business-like operations with public purposes

## Federal-State Relationship

### Federal Powers (Enumerated)
- Interstate commerce regulation
- Foreign affairs and defense
- Currency and banking
- Immigration and naturalization
- Postal system
- Intellectual property

### State Powers (Reserved)
- Education systems
- Law enforcement (criminal law)
- Public health and safety
- Local government oversight
- Professional licensing
- Family law

### Concurrent Powers (Shared)
- Taxation
- Law enforcement
- Environmental protection
- Transportation infrastructure

This system creates a complex but functional federal structure designed to balance power, protect rights, and serve citizen needs through multiple levels and branches of government.
