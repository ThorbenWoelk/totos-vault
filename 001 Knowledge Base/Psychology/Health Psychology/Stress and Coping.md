# Stress and Coping

Stress is loosely defined as a state of mental or emotional strain or tension resulting from adverse or very demanding circumstances.

## Mechanics of Stress
- **HPA Axis**: The hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis is the body's central stress response system. It controls levels of cortisol and other stress-related hormones.
- **Fight or Flight**: The physiological reaction that occurs in response to a perceived harmful event, attack, or threat to survival.
- **Chronic Stress**: Prolonged activation of the stress response can lead to wear and tear on the body (allostatic load).

## Coping Strategies
- **Problem-Focused Coping**: Targets the causes of stress in practical ways (e.g., problem-solving, time management).
- **Emotion-Focused Coping**: Involves trying to reduce the negative emotional responses associated with stress (e.g., meditation, distraction).

## Clinical Relevance
- Chronic stress is a major factor in **[[../Anxiety Disorders/Generalized Anxiety Disorder|GAD]]** and other anxiety disorders.
- Traumatic stress is the defining feature of **[[../Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorders/Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder|PTSD]]**.
