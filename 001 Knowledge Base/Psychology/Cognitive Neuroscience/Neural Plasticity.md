# Neural Plasticity

Neural plasticity (or neuroplasticity) is the ability of the nervous system to change its activity in response to intrinsic or extrinsic stimuli by reorganizing its structure, functions, or connections.

## Mechanisms
- **Synaptic Plasticity**: Changes in the strength of existing synapses (Long-Term Potentiation/Depression).
- **Structural Plasticity**: The brain's ability to change its physical structure as a result of learning (e.g., grey matter density changes).

## Relevance
- **Learning & Memory**: The biological basis of learning.
- **Recovery from Injury**: Essential for recovery after **[[Traumatic Brain Injury]]**.
- **Habit Formation**: Underlying mechanism for **[[Health Behavior Change]]**.
