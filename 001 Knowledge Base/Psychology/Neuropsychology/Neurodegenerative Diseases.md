# Neurodegenerative Diseases

Neurodegenerative diseases affect the body's activities, such as balance, movement, talking, breathing, and heart function. They are characterized by the progressive loss of structure or function of neurons, including death of neurons.

## Key Conditions
- **Alzheimer's Disease**: The most common form of dementia, characterized by memory loss and cognitive decline.
- **Parkinson's Disease**: Affects movement (tremors, rigidity) but can also have cognitive effects.
- **Frontotemporal Dementia**: Affects personality, behavior, and language.

## Assessment
- Neuropsychological testing is often required for differential diagnosis (see **[[Assessment and Diagnosis]]**).
