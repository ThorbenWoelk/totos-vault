# Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)

Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) is a disruption in the normal function of the brain that can be caused by a blow, bump, or jolt to the head, the head suddenly and violently hitting an object, or when an object pierces the skull and enters brain tissue.

## Functional Impairments
The location and severity of injury determine the nature of the deficit:
- **Frontal Lobe**: Deficits in **[[Executive Function]]**, personality changes, impulsivity.
- **Temporal Lobe**: Memory issues (see **[[Memory Systems]]**), language deficits.
- **Parietal Lobe**: Visuospatial difficulties.

## Severity
- **Mild TBI (Concussion)**: Brief change in mental status or consciousness.
- **Severe TBI**: Extended period of unconsciousness or amnesia after the injury.
