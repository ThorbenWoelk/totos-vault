# Somatic Symptom Disorder (Bodily Distress Disorder)

A disorder characterized by an extreme focus on physical symptoms that causes major emotional distress and problems functioning, distinct from the symptoms' medical cause (or lack thereof).

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Code**: 300.82
- **Essential Features**:
    - **One or more somatic symptoms** that are distressing or result in significant disruption of daily life.
    - **Excessive thoughts, feelings, or behaviors** related to the symptoms or associated health concerns, answering at least one of these:
        1.  Disproportionate and persistent thoughts about the seriousness of one's symptoms.
        2.  Persistently high level of anxiety about health or symptoms.
        3.  Excessive time and energy devoted to these symptoms or health concerns.
    - Although any one somatic symptom may not be continuously present, the state of being symptomatic is persistent (typically >6 months).

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Name**: **Bodily Distress Disorder** (Code 6C20)
- **Essential Features**:
    - Presence of **bodily symptoms** that are distressing to the individual.
    - **Excessive attention** directed toward the symptoms (e.g., persistent preoccupation with their severity or negative consequences), often leading to repeated contact with healthcare providers.
    - This excessive attention persists **despite appropriate clinical examination**, investigations, or reassurance from healthcare providers.
    - Symptoms are persistent (present on most days for at least **several months**).
    - Results in **significant impairment** in functioning.
- **Key Differences**:
    - **Terminology**: ICD-11 uses "Bodily Distress Disorder" to replace several somatoform categories.
    - **Medical Unexplained Symptoms**: Both systems have moved *away* from the requirement that symptoms be "medically unexplained." The diagnosis can be made even if a medical condition exists, provided the *reaction* to it is excessive and maladaptive.
    - **Qualifiers**: ICD-11 provides specifiers for **Mild** (impact on one domain), **Moderate** (multiple domains), and **Severe**.
