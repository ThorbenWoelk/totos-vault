# Illness Anxiety Disorder (Hypochondriasis)

A disorder characterized by excessive worry about having or developing a serious undiagnosed medical condition, despite having minimal or no somatic symptoms.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Code**: 300.7
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Preoccupation** with having or acquiring a serious illness.
    - **Somatic symptoms are not present** or, if present, are only mild in intensity. (If a medical condition is present, the preoccupation is clearly excessive/disproportionate).
    - **High level of anxiety** about health; the individual is easily alarmed about personal health status.
    - **Excessive health-related behaviors** (e.g., repeatedly checking body for signs of illness) or **maladaptive avoidance** (e.g., avoiding doctor appointments).
    - Illness preoccupation has been present for at least **6 months**.
    - Not better explained by another mental disorder (e.g., Somatic Symptom Disorder, Panic Disorder, OCD).

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Name**: **Hypochondriasis** (Code 6B23)
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Persistent preoccupation or fear** about the possibility of having one or more serious, progressive, or life-threatening illnesses.
    - This preoccupation is accompanied by:
        1.  **Repetitive and excessive health-related behaviors** (checking body, searching definitions, seeking repeated reassurance/consultations).
        2.  **Maladaptive avoidance behavior** related to health (e.g., avoiding medical visits due to fear).
    - **Significant distress or impairment** in functioning.
    - Specifiers for **Insight**: "with fair to good insight" vs. "with poor to absent insight".
- **Key Differences**:
    - **Terminology**: ICD-11 retains the traditional term "Hypochondriasis" within the Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders grouping (though typically cross-listed with Anxiety), whereas DSM-5 renamed it "Illness Anxiety Disorder" and placed it under Somatic Symptom and Related Disorders.
    - **Focus**: Both emphasize the *worry* rather than the *symptoms* (distinguishing it from Somatic Symptom Disorder/Bodily Distress Disorder).

## Clinical Context
- **Core Mechanism**: Catastrophic interpretation of normal bodily sensations.
- **Cycle**: Worry -> checking/assurance seeking -> temporary relief -> return of worry.
- **Treatment**: CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) is the gold standard, focusing on challenging health-related beliefs and reducing checking behaviors.
