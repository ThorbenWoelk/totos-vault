# Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD)

A childhood disorder characterized by a recurrent pattern of angry/irritable mood, argumentative/defiant behavior, or vindictiveness toward authority figures.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Code**: 313.81
- **Essential Features**:
    - A pattern of angry/irritable mood, argumentative/defiant behavior, or vindictiveness lasting at least **6 months**.
    - Evidenced by at least **4 symptoms** from the following categories, exhibited during interaction with at least one individual who is not a sibling:
        - **Angry/Irritable Mood**: Lose temper, touchy/easily annoyed, angry/resentful.
        - **Argumentative/Defiant Behavior**: Argues with authority figures/adults, actively defies/refuses to comply with rules/requests, deliberately annoys others, blames others for mistakes.
        - **Vindictiveness**: Has been spiteful or vindictive at least twice within the past 6 months.
    - **Distress/Impairment**: Associated with distress in the individual or others (family/peers) or impacts functioning.
    - **Exclusion**: Behaviors do not occur exclusively during psychotic, substance, depressive, or bipolar disorders.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Code**: 6C90.1
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Persistent pattern** (e.g., 6 months or more) of markedly defiant, disobedient, provocative, or spiteful behavior.
    - Occurs more frequently than typically observed in individuals of comparable age and developmental level.
    - Not restricted to interaction with siblings.
    - **Key behaviors**: frequent temper outbursts, persistent argumentativeness, defiance of rules, blaming others, deliberate annoyance, vindictiveness.
    - **Impairment**: Significant impairment in personal, family, social, or educational functioning.
    - **Specifiers**:
        - With chronic irritability-anger.
        - Without chronic irritability-anger.
        - With limited prosocial emotions (optional).
- **Key Differences**:
    - **Specifiers**: ICD-11 explicitly includes specifiers for "chronic irritability-anger", helping to distinguish phenotypes that might overlap with DSM-5's disruptive mood dysregulation disorder.

## Clinical Context
- **Developmental Course**: Often precedes Conduct Disorder, but many children with ODD do not progress to CD.
- **Comorbidity**: Highly comorbid with **ADHD**.
