# Conduct Disorder (CD)

A regular pattern of behavior in which the basic rights of others or major age-appropriate societal norms or rules are violated.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Code**: 312.81 (Childhood-onset) / 312.82 (Adolescent-onset) / 312.89 (Unspecified)
- **Essential Features**:
    - A repetitive and persistent pattern of behavior violating the rights of others or societal norms.
    - At least **3 of the following 15 criteria** in the past 12 months, with at least one present in the past 6 months:
        - **Aggression to People and Animals**: Bullies/threatens, initiates fights, uses a weapon, physically cruel to people/animals, stolen while confronting a victim, forced sexual activity.
        - **Destruction of Property**: Fire-setting, destruction of property.
        - **Deceitfulness or Theft**: Broken into house/car, lying ("conning"), stealing without confrontation (shoplifting).
        - **Serious Violations of Rules**: Staying out at night (before age 13), running away overnight (at least twice), school truancy (before age 13).
    - **Impairment**: Clinically significant impairment in social, academic, or occupational functioning.
    - **Specifier**: **With Limited Prosocial Emotions** (Callous-Unemotional traits): Lack of remorse/guilt, callous/lack of empathy, unconcerned about performance, shallow or deficient affect.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Name**: **Conduct-Dissocial Disorder** (Code 6C91)
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Repetitive and persistent pattern** of behavior violating basic rights of others or major age-appropriate societal norms/laws.
    - **Examples**: Aggression to people/animals, destruction of property, deceitfulness/theft, serious rule violations.
    - **Duration**: Enduring over a significant period (e.g., 12 months).
    - **Types**:
        - Childhood-onset (before age 10).
        - Adolescent-onset (after age 10).
    - **Specifier**: **With Limited Prosocial Emotions** is also a key specifier in ICD-11 to identify the "Callous-Unemotional" subgroup.
- **Key Differences**:
    - **Terminology**: "Conduct-Dissocial Disorder" in ICD-11 emphasizes the "dissocial" (antisocial) aspect.
    - **Subtypes**: ICD-11 can further categorize into "Socialized" (peer relationships intact) vs "Unsocialized" (solitary/rejected), preserving a distinction from ICD-10.

## Clinical Context
- **Progression**: Can be a precursor to **Antisocial Personality Disorder** in adulthood (diagnosis of APD requires evidence of CD before age 15).
- **Severity**: The "Limited Prosocial Emotions" specifier indicates a more severe, persistent, and heritable form of the disorder, often with poorer treatment outcomes.
