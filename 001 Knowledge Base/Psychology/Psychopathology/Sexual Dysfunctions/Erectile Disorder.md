# Erectile Disorder (Male Erectile Dysfunction)

A recurring inability to achieve or maintain an erection suitable for satisfactory sexual performance.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Code**: 602.3
- **Essential Features**:
    - At least one of the following symptoms must be experienced on **almost all or all (75-100%)** occasions of sexual activity:
        1.  Marked difficulty in obtaining an erection during sexual activity.
        2.  Marked difficulty in maintaining an erection until the completion of sexual activity.
        3.  Marked decrease in erectile rigidity.
    - **Duration**: Symptoms have persisted for approximately **6 months**.
    - **Distress**: Causes clinically significant distress in the individual.
    - **Exclusion**: Not better explained by a nonsexual mental disorder, severe relationship distress, or exclusively by the effects of a substance/medication or another medical condition.
- **Specifiers**:
    - **Lifelong** vs. **Acquired**.
    - **Generalized** vs. **Situational**.
    - **Severity**: Mild, Moderate, Severe.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Name**: **Male Erectile Dysfunction** (Code 6C41)
- **Essential Features**:
    - Inability or marked reduction in the ability to attain or sustain a penile erection of sufficient duration or rigidity for sexual activity.
    - Occurs despite the **desire** for sexual activity and **adequate sexual stimulation**.
    - **Pattern**: Episodic or persistent over at least **several months**.
    - **Distress**: Associated with clinically significant distress.
- **Key Differences**:
    - **Desire/Stimulation Clause**: ICD-11 explicitly requires that the failure occurs *despite* adequate desire and stimulation, distinguishing it from disorders of low desire.
    - **Etiology**: ICD-11 moves away from the strict "organic vs. non-organic" dichotomy, encouraging an integrated biopsychosocial view.

## Clinical Context
- **Etiology**: Often multifactorial (vascular, neurological, hormonal, and psychological).
- **Performance Anxiety**: A common maintaining factor where the fear of failure creates a self-fulfilling prophecy.
- **Relationship Impact**: Can lead to avoidance of intimacy and relationship strain.
