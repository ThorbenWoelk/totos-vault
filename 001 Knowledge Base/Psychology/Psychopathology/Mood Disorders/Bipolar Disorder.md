# Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a brain disorder that causes changes in a person's mood, energy, and ability to function. People with bipolar disorder experience intense emotional states called "mood episodes": manic/hypomanic episodes (abnormally happy or irritable mood) and depressive episodes (sad mood).

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Bipolar I Disorder**: Defined by manic episodes that last at least 7 days, or by manic symptoms that are so severe that the person needs immediate hospital care. Usually, depressive episodes occur as well, typically lasting at least 2 weeks.
    - **Manic Episode**: A distinct period of abnormally and persistently elevated, expansive, or irritable mood and abnormally and persistently increased activity or energy, lasting at least 1 week and present most of the day, nearly every day (or any duration if hospitalization is necessary).
- **Bipolar II Disorder**: Defined by a pattern of depressive episodes and hypomanic episodes, but not the full-blown manic episodes described above.
    - **Hypomanic Episode**: Same symptoms as manic episode but lasting at least 4 consecutive days and not severe enough to cause marked impairment in social or occupational functioning or to necessitate hospitalization.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Bipolar Type I Disorder (6A60)**:
    - Defined by the occurrence of one or more **Manic Episodes** or **Mixed Episodes**.
    - **Manic Episode**: Extreme mood state (euphoria, irritability, expansiveness) accompanied by increased activity or energy, lasting at least 1 week (or less if hospitalized).
    - **Mixed Episode**: Characterized by the presence of several prominent manic and depressive symptoms occurring simultaneously or alternating rapidly, lasting at least 2 weeks.
- **Bipolar Type II Disorder (6A61)**:
    - Characterized by the occurrence of one or more **Hypomanic Episodes** and at least one **Depressive Episode**.
    - **Hypomanic Episode**: Persistent elevation of mood or irritability and increased activity, lasting at least "several days", without marked impairment.
- **Key Differences from DSM-5**:
    - **Mixed Episode**: ICD-11 retains the "Mixed Episode" as a distinct diagnostic episode for Bipolar I, whereas DSM-5 replaced this with a "with mixed features" specifier applied to manic or depressive episodes.
    - **Hypomania Duration**: ICD-11 specifies "several days" vs DSM-5's strict "4 consecutive days".


## Differential Diagnosis
- Distinguishing from **[[Depression|Major Depressive Disorder]]** can be difficult if the patient presents during a depressive episode.
