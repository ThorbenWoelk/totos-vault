# Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD is a psychiatric disorder that may occur in people who have experienced or witnessed a traumatic event such as a natural disaster, a serious accident, a terrorist act, war/combat, or rape/sexual violence.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Criterion A**: Exposure to actual or threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence.
- **Criterion B**: Presence of one or more intrusion symptoms associated with the traumatic event(s) (e.g., memes, nightmares, flashbacks).
- **Criterion C**: Persistent avoidance of stimuli associated with the traumatic event(s).
- **Criterion D**: Negative alterations in cognitions and mood associated with the traumatic event(s) (e.g., inability to remember key aspects of the trauma, persistent negative beliefs about oneself).
- **Criterion E**: Marked alterations in arousal and reactivity associated with the traumatic event(s) (e.g., irritability, hypervigilance).
- **Criterion F**: Duration of the disturbance is more than 1 month.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Code**: 6B40
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Stressor**: Exposure to an extremely threatening or horrific event or series of events.
    - **Core Symptom Clusters** (all 3 required):
        1.  **Re-experiencing**: Vivid intrusive memories, flashbacks, or nightmares with strong emotions/physical sensations.
        2.  **Avoidance**: Persistent avoidance of impulses, thoughts, memories, situations, or people reminiscent of the event.
        3.  **Heightened Sense of Threat**: Hypervigilance or enhanced startle reaction to present surroundings (e.g., unexpected noises).
    - Symptoms persist for at least **several weeks** and cause significant impairment.
- **Key Differences from DSM-5**:
    - **Simpler Structure**: Diagnosis relies on 3 core clusters (Re-experiencing, Avoidance, Threat) instead of DSM-5's 4 clusters.
    - **Specific Exclusions**: ICD-11 excludes the "Negative Alterations in Cognitions and Mood" cluster found in DSM-5 (these symptoms might often fall under Depression or C-PTSD in ICD-11 context).
    - **Duration**: "Several weeks" vs DSM-5's ">1 month".
