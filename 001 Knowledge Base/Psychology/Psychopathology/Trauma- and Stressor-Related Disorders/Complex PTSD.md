# Complex PTSD (C-PTSD)

Complex PTSD is a condition that results from chronic, long-term exposure to emotional trauma over which a victim has little or no control and from which there is little or no hope of escape.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Code**: 6B41
- **Status**: Distinct formal diagnosis in ICD-11 (Not in DSM-5, which uses "PTSD" with possible dissociative specifiers or considers comorbid personality issues).
- **Essential Features**:
    1.  **Meets all criteria for PTSD (6B40)** (Re-experiencing, Avoidance, Heightened Threat).
    2.  **Disturbances in Self-Organization (DSO)** (All 3 required):
        - **Affect Dysregulation**: Emotional reactivity, anger outbursts, emotional numbing, or difficulty experiencing positive emotions.
        - **Negative Self-Concept**: Persistent beliefs about oneself as diminished, defeated, or worthless; deep feelings of shame or guilt.
        - **Disturbances in Relationships**: Difficulties in sustaining relationships and in feeling close to others.
- **Context**: Typically follows severe, prolonged, or repetitive trauma from which escape is difficult or impossible (e.g., torture, childhood abuse, domestic violence).


## Connections
- Often shares features with [[Borderline Personality Disorder]], leading to debates about differential diagnosis.
