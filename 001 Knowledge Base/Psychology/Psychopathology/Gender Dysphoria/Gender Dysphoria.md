# Gender Dysphoria (Gender Incongruence)

A marked incongruence between an individual's experienced/expressed gender and their assigned gender.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Name**: **Gender Dysphoria** (Code 302.85 for Adolescents/Adults)
- **Classification**: Listed under its own chapter "Gender Dysphoria" within the manual of Mental Disorders.
- **Essential Features (Adolescents/Adults)**:
    - A marked incongruence between one’s experienced/expressed gender and assigned gender, of at least **6 months** duration.
    - Manifested by at least 2 of the following:
        1.  Incongruence between experienced gender and primary/secondary sex characteristics.
        2.  Strong desire to be rid of one's sex characteristics.
        3.  Strong desire for the sex characteristics of the other gender.
        4.  Strong desire to be of the other gender.
        5.  Strong desire to be treated as the other gender.
        6.  Strong conviction that one has the typical feelings/reactions of the other gender.
    - **Distress/Impairment**: The condition is associated with clinically significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Name**: **Gender Incongruence** (Code HA60 for Adolescence/Adulthood)
- **Classification**: **MOVED** out of the "Mental and Behavioural Disorders" chapter entirely. It is now located in a new chapter: **"Conditions related to sexual health"**.
- **Essential Features**:
    - A marked and persistent incongruence between an individual’s experienced gender and the assigned sex.
    - Often leads to a desire to "transition" (hormonal treatment, surgery) to live and be accepted as a person of the experienced gender.
    - **Duration**: At least **several months** (for adolescents/adults).
    - **No Distress Requirement**: Unlike DSM-5, a diagnosis of Gender Incongruence **does NOT require** the presence of significant distress or impairment. The diagnosis is based on the incongruence itself.
- **Key Differences**:
    - **Depathologization**: The shift from "Psychiatric Disorder" to "Sexual Health Condition" is a major conceptual change aimed at reducing stigma while maintaining access to gender-affirming healthcare (which often requires a diagnosis code for insurance).
    - **Distress**: DSM-5 diagnoses the *distress* (dysphoria); ICD-11 diagnoses the *incongruence*.

## Clinical Context
- **Treatment**: The standard of care is gender-affirming care (social transition, hormone therapy, surgery) rather than "reparative" therapies (which are considered unethical).
- **Minority Stress**: High rates of anxiety/depression in this population are often attributed to societal stigma and discrimination ("Minority Stress Model") rather than the gender identity itself.
