# Anorexia Nervosa

Anorexia nervosa is an eating disorder characterized by weight loss (or lack of appropriate weight gain in growing children); difficulties maintaining an appropriate body weight for height, age, and stature; and, in many individuals, distorted body image.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Criterion A**: Restriction of energy intake relative to requirements, leading to a significantly low body weight in the context of age, sex, developmental trajectory, and physical health.
- **Criterion B**: Intense fear of gaining weight or of becoming fat, or persistent behavior that interferes with weight gain, even though at a significantly low weight.
- **Criterion C**: Disturbance in the way in which one's body weight or shape is experienced, undue influence of body weight or shape on self-evaluation, or persistent lack of recognition of the seriousness of the current low body weight.

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Code**: 6B80
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Significantly low body weight** for height, age, and developmental stage (e.g., BMI < 18.5 kg/m² in adults; BMI-for-age < 5th percentile in children).
    - **Persistent behaviors** to prevent weight gain (restricting intake, purging, excessive exercise).
    - **Low body weight/shape is central to self-evaluation** or inaccurately perceived as normal.
- **Key Differences from DSM-5**:
    - **BMI Thresholds**: ICD-11 provides specific BMI guidance (e.g., differentiating "significantly low" vs "dangerously low").
    - **Removal of "Atypical"**: Concepts like "atypical anorexia" are better integrated, reducing the use of "Other Specified" categories compared to DSM-5.
    - **Amenorrhea**: Like DSM-5, ICD-11 has removed the requirement for amenorrhea.


## Subtypes
- **Restricting Type**: Weight loss is accomplished primarily through dieting, fasting, and/or excessive exercise.
- **Binge-Eating/Purging Type**: The individual has engaged in recurrent episodes of binge eating or purging behavior.
