# Binge Eating Disorder (BED)

An eating disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of eating large quantities of food (often very quickly and to the point of discomfort), a feeling of a loss of control during the binge, and distinct from Bulimia Nervosa by the *absence* of regular compensatory behaviors.

## Diagnosis (DSM-5 Criteria)
- **Code**: 307.51
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Recurrent episodes of binge eating** characterized by both:
        1.  Eating, in a discrete period (e.g., 2 hours), an amount of food that is definitely larger than most people would eat.
        2.  A sense of **lack of control** over eating during the episode.
    - **Associated Features** (3 or more required):
        - Eating much more rapidly than normal.
        - Eating until feeling uncomfortably full.
        - Eating large amounts when not physically hungry.
        - Eating alone because of embarrassment.
        - Feeling disgusted with oneself, depressed, or very guilty afterward.
    - **Marked distress** regarding the binge eating.
    - **Frequency**: At least **once a week** for **3 months**.
    - **No Compensaatory Behaviors**: Not associated with recurrent use of inappropriate compensatory behaviors (e.g., purging, fasting, excessive exercise).

## Diagnosis (ICD-11 Criteria)
- **Code**: 6B82
- **Essential Features**:
    - **Frequent, recurrent episodes of binge eating**:
        - Subjective loss of control.
        - Eating notably more or differently than usual.
        - Feeling unable to stop or limit intake.
    - **Distress**: Experienced as very distressing; accompanied by negative emotions (guilt, disgust).
    - **No Compensatory Behaviors**: Not followed by purging/compensatory acts (distinguishing it from 6B81 Bulimia Nervosa).
    - **Impairment**: Significant impairment in personal, family, social, educational, or occupational functioning.
    - **Frequency**: "Once a week or more" over usually "several months" (but can be diagnosed after 1 month if very frequent).
- **Key Differences from DSM-5**:
    - **"Subjective" Binges**: ICD-11 generally allows for a diagnosis based on the *subjective* sense of loss of control even if the amount of food isn't "objectively" massive (though "notably more" is the phrase). DSM-5 strictly requires an "amount definitely larger than most people would eat."
    - **Duration**: ICD-11 is more flexible ("several months" but allows 1 month), whereas DSM-5 strictly requires 3 months.
    - **Symptom Count**: DSM-5 lists 5 specific behavioral indicators (rapid, alone, guilt, etc.) and requires 3. ICD-11 focuses more on the *core concept* of loss of control and distress without a rigid checkbox of associated behaviors.

## Clinical Context
- **Prevalence**: The most common eating disorder in the general population.
- **Comorbidity**: High rates of comorbidity with depression, anxiety, and substance use disorders.
- **Health Consequences**: Associated with obesity, diabetes, hypertension, and chronic pain conditions.
