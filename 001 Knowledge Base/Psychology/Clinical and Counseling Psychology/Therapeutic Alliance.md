# Therapeutic Alliance

The therapeutic alliance (also known as the working alliance) refers to the collaborative and affective bond between therapist and client. It is widely considered the most important common factor in predicting successful therapy outcomes.

## Components
1.  **Bond**: The personal connection and trust between therapist and client.
2.  **Tasks**: Agreement on the specific activities that will happen in therapy.
3.  **Goals**: Agreement on the objectives of the therapy.

## Ethics
Establishing a strong alliance requires strict adherence to ethical principles, including:
- Confidentiality
- Informed Consent
- Maintaining appropriate boundaries (avoiding dual relationships).
