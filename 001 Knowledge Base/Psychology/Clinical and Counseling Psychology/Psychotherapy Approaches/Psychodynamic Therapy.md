# Psychodynamic Therapy

Psychodynamic therapy focuses on unconscious processes as they are manifested in the client's present behavior. The goals of psychodynamic therapy are client self-awareness and understanding of the influence of the past on present behavior.

## Core Concepts
- **Unconscious Motivation**: Feelings and behavior are influenced by unconscious motives.
- **Defense Mechanisms**: Psychological strategies used to cope with reality and maintain self-image (e.g., repression, projection).
- **Transference**: The redirection of feelings and desires and especially of those unconsciously retained from childhood toward a new object (often the therapist).
- **Past Influence**: Early childhood experiences are crucial in shaping adult personality.

## Theoretical Foundations
- **[[Attachment Theory|Attachment Theory]]** provides a key framework for understanding relationship patterns in psychodynamic work.

## Applications
Often used for:
- **[[Depression]]**
- **[[Borderline Personality Disorder]]** (often via specialized forms like Transference-Focused Psychotherapy)
- Somatic symptom disorders
