## When is a debate worth my time? 

When it's not reasonable to argue too much: 
- If the debate is trying to answer an empirical question - such as whether men are better leaders than women - then it should ultimately be settled by evidence, not rhetoric. 
- For such testable claims, and especially if dozens of high-quality studies and meta-analyses already exist, a debate between two speakers is not an appropriate way to determine the answer. We wouldn’t settle questions like whether smoking is harmful or vaccines are effective through a public debate. What we could do is communicate the current conclusive state of science. 

A debate is still worthwhile, if:
- the implications of the evidence are up for debate. for example, whether companies should introduce quotas, how leaders should be selected, or which leadership traits should be encouraged. 
- These are normative or political questions where science informs the discussion but does not dictate the answer.
